Motivational Songs to Listen to when You Need to Amp up the Girl Power ...

“Beautiful” by Christina Aguilera • “Fly” by Nicki Minaj Feat. Rihanna • “Girl in a Country Song” by Maddie and Tae • “Girl on Fire” by Alicia Keys • “Perfect” by Pink • More ...

By Lyndsie

When life has you feeling down and you need an epic boost of confidence, there’s nothing quite like jamming out to some motivational songs to get you back on your feet. Whether you need an extra push to tackle that intimidating project at work, or just a reminder of your own fierce power, the right music can make all the difference. And let's be real, sometimes you just need to turn up the volume and shout the lyrics at the top of your lungs! As we step deeper into 2024, it's all about embracing your strength, celebrating your journey, and finding the anthems that resonate with your soul.

Ladies, do you remember the last time you belted out "Roar" by Katy Perry or strutted around your living room to “Run the World (Girls)” by Beyoncé? These songs are more than just catchy tunes; they are anthems of empowerment. Seriously, there’s nothing like a classic girl power anthem to remind you that you are unstoppable. I still recall getting ready for my graduation with Beyoncé blasting in my ears, thinking, "Yes, I can conquer the world!"

And it’s not just about the beats-it’s the lyrics too. You see, these empowering lyrics act as the perfect pep talk. Songs like "Fight Song" by Rachel Platten and "Brave" by Sara Bareilles are like having a best friend who believes in you, even when you’re feeling low. Need proof? Just check out our list of top motivational songs that have the power to transform your mindset.

Let's talk playlists for a sec. It might be a sunny Sunday morning, you're feeling lazy, and the only thing urging you out of bed is the knowledge that a cup of coffee and some great music await you. This is where creating the ultimate empowering playlist becomes your secret weapon. Trust me, curating this list can be as therapeutic as listening to it. With each song, you’re reminded of why you chose it-whether it’s a personal experience, a memorable moment, or just because it makes you feel like a badass!

But don’t just take my word for it. In tips for creating your own girl power playlist, we delve deeper into how to build a collection that speaks to you personally. From layering in some lesser-known gems to adding those top chart-toppers, your playlist can become a personal manifesto of encouragement and positivity.

So, if you find yourself needing a bit of an uplift, remember that the right song can be your best ally. Whether it’s the driving beat of a pop classic or the soul-stirring words of an acoustic melody, motivational songs are your ticket to reclaiming your power and walking through 2024 with your head held high.

1. “Beautiful” by Christina Aguilera

I confess, I'm not an enormous Xtina fan, but I will never deny that her voice is fierce or that she has a number of truly amazing songs. This is one of them, and it is hands down one of my favorites. The message is so incredibly important. I think this song should be required listening for every woman, every teenager, and every little girl in the world – just so they know that they are beautiful.

2. “Fly” by Nicki Minaj Feat. Rihanna

I am in love with these two women. Nicki gets a lot of shit and I know she says some very questionable things, but if you take the time to listen to her interviews, her comments, and the songs that don't make it to the radio, you might just see a different side of her. And Rihanna, well, I wish we could get married, but that's not the point. The point is that “Fly” will make you want to spread your wings and do just that.

3. “Girl in a Country Song” by Maddie and Tae

This song … this song is just amazeballs. AMAZEBALLS. The lyrics are so on point – sharp, funny, and relevant. Even if you don't like country, this belongs on your girl power playlist. My favorite lyric? “'Cause I got a name/ And to you it ain't 'pretty little thing,' 'honey,' or 'baby.'”

4. “Girl on Fire” by Alicia Keys

Alicia is most definitely a girl on fire. The second you hear the opening notes of this song, you'll be a girl on fire, too. In fact, you are a girl on fire – right now, right this second, and always. You, too, can have your feet on the ground and your head in the clouds, and all the while, you can rule the world.

5. “Perfect” by Pink

Pink's catalog is full of empowering songs that speak to women, but this is one of my favorites. There are moments when you feel like so much less than what you are. In those moments, you need a song like this. Your flaws don't matter. Your imperfections do not define you. They don't matter because they make you who you are, and perfection is an unreachable goal, anyway.

6. “Run the World (Girls)” by Beyonce

So, obviously, you can include almost all of Lemonade on your girl power playlist. However, this is a must-have track, too – because you can rule the world. No matter who you are or what you decide to do with your life, you can own it, live it, love it, and make it yours.

7. “Stronger” by Kelly Clarkson

This is practically a girl power anthem by this point, for good reason. It's impossible to listen to this song and not feel uplifted and powerful. Kelly knows.

8. “Skyscraper” by Demi Lovato

I love this song for all sorts of reasons. Mainly, I think something about it speaks to all the pressures that girls and women feel, all day, every day. What do you think?

Don't forget to share your own empowering favorites. Tell us the songs that make you feel like a goddess, like you're larger than life, and like you're capable of everything.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are some good motivational songs for women?
Oh, there are so many great ones! 'Girl on Fire' by Alicia Keys, 'Run the World (Girls)' by Beyoncé, and 'Fight Song' by Rachel Platten are some of my favorites. They always give me that extra boost of confidence!
Why are girl power songs so popular?
You know, there's just something really powerful about music that uplifts and celebrates women. These songs remind us of our strength, our resilience, and our ability to overcome anything. Plus, they can be so much fun to sing along to!
Can you suggest some positive songs that girls would love?
Absolutely! 'Roar' by Katy Perry, 'Brave' by Sara Bareilles, and 'I'm Every Woman' by Chaka Khan are all fantastic choices. These tunes are full of positive vibes and empowering messages.
Are there any newer girl power anthems I should check out?
For sure! Songs like 'Good as Hell' by Lizzo, 'Confident' by Demi Lovato, and 'Juice' by Lizzo are newer tracks that definitely pack a punch. They really get you in the mood to take on the world!
What makes a song empowering for women?
Great question! It's all about the lyrics and the vibe. Empowering songs often have messages about self-love, strength, independence, and perseverance. They make you feel strong and confident, like you can accomplish anything!
