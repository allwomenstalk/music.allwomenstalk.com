Best Websites to Use when Purchasing Concert Tickets Online ...

Ticket Master • Stub Hub • Live Nation • AEG Live • Seat Geek • More ...

By Holly

It doesn't matter if you're dying to see a Broadway show, your favorite band, or a sporting event. No matter what kind of show you want to see, their are sites that provide tickets for the experience. Of course, everything comes with its price. Since you want to get the best deals that you can, you should make a point to check out each one of the sites on this list before you actually make your purchase:

1. Ticket Master

Ticketmaster.com is probably the first site that pops into your mind when you want to see a concert. It sells tickets for shows, for circuses, for sporting events, for stand-up comedians, and for anything else you could ever want a ticket for. The site even lets you sign up for email alerts for specific singers and bands, so you can be notified as soon as tickets for the people you love go on sale.

2. Stub Hub

Stubhub.com lets you do more than buy tickets. It also allows you to sell tickets. If you have a friend that is always going to concerts and festivals, then you can even buy them a gift card for the site. It will be sent right to their email, so it makes for the perfect last minute gift.

3. Live Nation

Livenation.com asks you to enter a city name and then will provide you with a list of bands that are playing near you. Once in a while, it will even provide you with bonus items that you can add to your cart. For instance, if you try to buy Van Halen tickets, the site will ask you if you want to add a "Live Tokyo Dome In Concert Album & Classic Logo Tee" to your order.

4. AEG Live

Aeglive.com will help you search for events, concerts, and even festivals. It'll alert you about the hottest things in town, so you won't have to worry about missing out on a fun experience. The site even lets you search through categories like "classical," "family events," and "electronic."

5. Seat Geek

Seatgeek.com will ask you to type in what artist, event, or venue you're looking for. Even if there aren't any tickets available for the band or artist at that time, the site will recommend people that are similar, so you can still go somewhere to have a great time.

6. Ticket Lodge

Like most of these other sites, ticketlodge.com will let you look through tickets for concerts, sporting events, and broadway shows. However, it also has a blog that contains some fascinating articles about upcoming tours. It's a great place to get all your music news.

7. Ticket City

Even though ticketcity.com caters more to sport fans than music fans, it still contains tickets for some great concerts. If you want to buy tickets to see Taylor Swift and tickets for a hockey game for your boyfriend at the same time, then this site will take care of all of your needs.

Whenever you want to buy tickets for a concert, make sure you shop around to get the best deals that you can. As long as the concert isn't in danger of selling out anytime soon, there's no reason to buy tickets without seeing what seats and prices you can get elsewhere. Frequently Asked Questions

What are some safe websites to buy concert tickets?
Trusted sites like Ticketmaster, StubHub, and SeatGeek offer secure purchases and verified tickets.
Can I use mobile apps to buy concert tickets?
Yes, many ticket platforms have mobile apps for easy browsing and buying on the go.
Are there websites that help find cheap concert tickets?
Sites like Vivid Seats and Ticketswap often have discounted tickets or resale options at lower prices.
How do I avoid ticket scams online?
Stick to official or well-known resale platforms, check seller reviews, and avoid deals that seem too good to be true.
Is it better to buy tickets directly from the venue's website?
Buying from the venue usually guarantees legitimate tickets and may offer exclusive options.
Can I choose my seat online before purchase?
Most sites, like Ticketmaster and SeatGeek, provide interactive seat maps to select your preferred seats.
Do websites charge extra fees for concert tickets?
Yes, many ticket sites add service or processing fees, so compare total costs before buying.
Can I resell my concert tickets on these websites?
Platforms like StubHub and Vivid Seats allow you to resell tickets securely if your plans change.
Are electronic tickets accepted at concert venues?
Most venues accept mobile or electronic tickets, but check the event details to be sure.
What should I do if my concert ticket website crashes during a sale?
Try refreshing your browser, use the app if available, or check other official ticket sellers quickly to secure tickets.
