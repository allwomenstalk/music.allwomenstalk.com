# Best Websites to Use when Purchasing Concert Tickets Online ...

It doesn't matter if you're dying to see a Broadway show, your favorite band, or a sporting event. No matter what kind of show you want to see, their are sites that provide tickets for the experience. Of course, everything comes with its price. Since you want to get the best deals that you can, you should make a point to check out each one of the sites on this list before you actually make your purchase:

## 1. Ticket Master

[Ticketmaster.com](http://www.Ticketmaster.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com) is probably the first site that pops into your mind when you want to see a concert. It sells tickets for shows, for circuses, for sporting events, for stand-up comedians, and for anything else you could ever want a ticket for. The site even lets you sign up for email alerts for specific singers and bands, so you can be notified as soon as tickets for the people you love go on sale.

## 2. Stub Hub

[Stubhub.com](http://www.Stubhub.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com) lets you do more than buy tickets. It also allows you to sell tickets. If you have a friend that is always going to concerts and festivals, then you can even buy them a gift card for the site. It will be sent right to their email, so it makes for the perfect last minute gift.

## 3. Live Nation

[Livenation.com](http://www.Livenation.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com) asks you to enter a city name and then will provide you with a list of bands that are playing near you. Once in a while, it will even provide you with bonus items that you can add to your cart. For instance, if you try to buy Van Halen tickets, the site will ask you if you want to add a "Live Tokyo Dome In Concert Album & Classic Logo Tee" to your order.

## 4. AEG Live

[Aeglive.com](https://www.aegpresents.com/) will help you search for events, concerts, and even festivals. It'll alert you about the hottest things in town, so you won't have to worry about missing out on a fun experience. The site even lets you search through categories like "classical," "family events," and "electronic."

## 5. Seat Geek

[Seatgeek.com](https://seatgeek.com/?ranMID=47126&ranEAID=je6NUbpObpQ&ranSiteID=je6NUbpObpQ-AkK67uNI8KDeEkkBqswDNg&aid=15923) will ask you to type in what artist, event, or venue you're looking for. Even if there aren't any tickets available for the band or artist at that time, the site will recommend people that are similar, so you can still go somewhere to have a great time.

## 6. Ticket Lodge

Like most of these other sites, [ticketlodge.com](https://www.ticketlodge.com/) will let you look through tickets for concerts, sporting events, and broadway shows. However, it also has a blog that contains some fascinating articles about upcoming tours. It's a great place to get all your music news.

## 7. Ticket City

Even though [ticketcity.com](https://www.ticketcity.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com) caters more to sport fans than music fans, it still contains tickets for some great concerts. If you want to buy tickets to see Taylor Swift and tickets for a hockey game for your boyfriend at the same time, then this site will take care of all of your needs.

Whenever you want to buy tickets for a concert, make sure you shop around to get the best deals that you can. As long as the concert isn't in danger of selling out anytime soon, there's no reason to buy tickets without seeing what seats and prices you can get elsewhere.
