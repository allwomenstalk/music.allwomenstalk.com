7 Songs to Listen to when You're Fed up with Love ...

Girls Don't like Boys by Good Charlotte • Heart Attack by Demi Lovato • How to Be a Heartbreaker by Marina and the Diamonds • Problem by Ariana Grande • Just like You by Three Days Grace • More ...

By Holly

There are helpful songs to listen to when you're fed up with love. If you've recently gone through a breakup, or can't get your crush's attention, put on some music to soothe you. Here are some of the best songs to listen to when you're fed up with love:

1. Girls Don't like Boys by Good Charlotte

There's much more to life than relationships. This is one of the best songs to listen to when you're fed up with love, because it reminds you that boys aren't the only thing in the world. Why waste your time chasing after someone when you can do something more valuable with your time? Make sure that you have your head on straight, because not all boys will treat you right.

2. Heart Attack by Demi Lovato

Love is an amazing thing, but it can be terrifying to think about. Demi knows how vulnerable love can make you, so she makes sure to put her defenses up. Pretty much everyone can relate to the way she feels about feelings. Who needs them, right?

3. How to Be a Heartbreaker by Marina and the Diamonds

Even if you hate this song, the half naked men in the video will make you love the video. Of course, it's hard to ignore the lyrics when they're so spot on. If you want to break hearts, follow her advice, and don't get attached to anyone. She knows exactly what to do when you're not in the mood to feel like you're in a sappy romance film.

4. Problem by Ariana Grande

If you've turned on the radio once during the last few months, you should already know this song. It's so popular, because it's catchy and gives good advice. Don't be upset when a guy leaves your life. Be excited that you have one less problem to deal with. It'll make your life much easier.

5. Just like You by Three Days Grace

Take a moment to really think about the person you're upset over. Whether you realize it or not, they have flaws. Do you really want to be anything like them? If someone doesn't wish to be part of your life, then it's their loss. You don't need them.

6. I Hate Myself for Loving You by Joan Jett

Sometimes we fall for the wrong person, and we can't do anything about it. We know we should forget about them, but it's impossible to do so. No matter what they do, you can't stop loving them, and you hate yourself for it. If you're in a situation like this, blast Joan Jett, and try to forget all about your worries.

7. When I Get Home You're so Dead by Mayday Parade

This song shows that girls make boys cry, and vice versa. When you fall for someone, you're allowing yourself to get hurt. Of course, it's impossible to go through life without feeling a bit of pain, so don't swear off of relationships completely.

Music can make you realize that you're not the only person in a certain situation, and help you get through it. So put on your headphones, choose one of these songs, and raise the volume as high as you can stand. What song do you listen to when you're annoyed with your crush, partner, or love in general?

Frequently Asked Questions

Why would someone want to listen to songs about being fed up with love?
Sometimes, music helps us process our feelings. When you're tired of love, these songs can be really comforting and make you feel less alone.
Can listening to 'fed up with love' songs make you feel better?
Totally! It’s like having a friend who just gets it. It can be cathartic and help you release some of that pent-up emotion.
Are there specific genres that these 'fed up with love' songs belong to?
Not really, you can find these themes in all kinds of music—pop, rock, country, you name it. There's something out there for everyone.
Can you recommend any other types of music for when you're feeling down about love?
Sure! Soulful ballads, empowering anthems, or even some upbeat tracks to lift your spirits can work great too. It depends on what you need at the moment.
Is it normal to feel better after listening to these songs?
Absolutely. Music has a powerful way of helping us work through emotions. Feeling better after listening is just a sign that it's doing its job.
