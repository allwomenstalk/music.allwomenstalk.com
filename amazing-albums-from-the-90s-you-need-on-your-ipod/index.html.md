# 7 Amazing Albums from the 90s You Need on Your IPod ...

The 90s spawned some of the greatest music of all time, and if you don’t have these 7 amazing albums from the 90s on your iPod, it is time to start downloading! There are hundreds of albums from 90s that could have made this list, but I chose to highlight 7 of my favorites. Whether you are into grunge rock, hip hop, or alternative rock, there is something on this list of amazing albums from the 90s for everyone to enjoy!

## 1. Nevermind – Nirvana

[itunes.apple.com](https://music.apple.com/us/album/nevermind/1440783617)

One of the most amazing albums from the 90s is Nirvana’s Nevermind. This album is constantly ranked as one of the greatest albums of all time. Nevermind helped bring alternative music mainstream. The grunge album has been certified Diamond and has sold more than 30 million copies worldwide! All the best known Nirvana songs are on Nevermind, including “Smells Like Teen Spirit,” “Lithium,” “Come as You Are,” and “In Bloom.”

## 2. Rage against the Machine – Rage against the Machine

[itunes.apple.com](https://goo.gl/eHSXN6)

Rage Against the Machine’s (RATM) self-titled album is without a doubt one of the best albums of the 1990s. RATM was one of the first commercially successful metal rap albums. You will love the guitar rifts on the album, and after listening, you will see why it is loved by fans and critics alike. RATM features the tracks “Killing in the Name,” “Bombtrack,” and “Know Your Enemy.”

## 3. Ready to Die – the Notorious B.I.G

[itunes.apple.com](https://music.apple.com/us/album/ready-to-die-the-remaster/204669326)

Ready to Die is the debut album from Biggie. This album is not just one of the amazing albums from the 90s, it is considered to be one of the greatest albums of all time. Its influence in the hip hop and rap world is immeasurable. Ready to Die is the only album that was released during Biggie’s lifetime, as he died shortly before the release of his second album. Although he only has two albums to his name, The Notorious B.I.G. left an everlasting legacy with his work. The album contains hits such as “Big Poppa” and “Juicy.”

## 4. Jagged Little Pill – Alanis Morissette

[itunes.apple.com](https://music.apple.com/us/album/jagged-little-pill/79989523)

Jagged Little Pill is one of my all time favorite albums. The album was the third release for recording artist, Alanis Morissette. The album received critical acclaim and remains Alanis’s most influential album to date. After all, who doesn’t love singing along to the smash hit off the album, “You Oughta Know?”

## 5. Third Eye Blind – Third Eye Blind

[itunes.apple.com](https://music.apple.com/us/album/third-eye-blind/271978738)

Third Eye Blind’s debut, self-titled album is full of great 90s jams. One interesting thing about the album is that it only peaked at #25 on the Billboard charts, but it remained on the chars for over 100 weeks! The album produced many hits, including three songs that were in the Top 10 on the Billboard Charts: “Semi-Charmed Life,” “Jumper,” and “How’s It Going to Be.”

## 6. All Eyez on Me – 2Pac

[itunes.apple.com](https://music.apple.com/us/album/all-eyez-on-me-remastered/6917199)

All Eyez on Me is 2Pac’s fourth studio album, and arguably his best. The album has sold over 5 million copies worldwide since its 1996 release. All Eyez on Me is known as one of the greatest hip hop albums of all time. The album is known for the hits “California Love” and “2 of Amerikaz Most Wanted.”

## 7. Metallica – Metallica

[itunes.apple.com](https://goo.gl/An1aHw)

Metallica’s self-titled album, sometimes called The Black album, is one of the amazing albums from the 90s you need on your iPod. The album has sold over 16 million copies in the US alone and over 30 million copies worldwide. Metallica was the first album to reach the top of the charts for the band. Metallica features the hit songs “Enter Sandman,” “Nothing Else Matters,” and “Wherever I May Roam.”

I love each of these amazing albums from the 90s and believe every person should have them on their iPod. These are not only some of the best albums from the decade, they are some of the greatest albums of all time. What are some of your favorite albums from the 90s? Give us your input in the comments!
